# What is HULU.COM on my credit card statement?

                This is a charge from Hulu for a streaming subscription. You signed up for on-demand TV shows and movies, and the monthly or annual fee auto-renewed.

## Full Name & What It Is

            HULU.COM is the billing code for Hulu, one of the biggest streaming services in the world. Plans: Hulu with ads ($7.99/mo), Hulu without ads ($17.99/mo), Hulu + Live TV ($81.99/mo with ads, $89.99/mo without). Student plan: $1.99/mo. Many users see this charge because Hulu offers 30-day free trials — you provide your card to start, and if you forget to cancel, the paid subscription kicks in automatically. The Disney Bundle can also cause confusion, as charges may appear under individual service names.

                Merchant Code: HULU.COM

                Company: Hulu, LLC (owned by Disney)

                Service: Hulu Streaming Subscription

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Free Trial Forgotten**You signed up for a free month of Hulu to watch a new movie release and forgot to cancel before the trial ended.

                **2. Student Plan Auto-Renew**Your college student used your credit card to get the $1.99 student deal, and it auto-renews every month.

                **3. Disney Bundle Confusion**You subscribed to a Disney+ and Hulu bundle, and a recent price increase made the HULU.COM portion stand out more.

                **4. Smart TV Reactivation**A guest or family member accidentally reactivated a cancelled subscription while trying to log in on a shared TV.

                **5. Promo Price Expired**You signed up for a Black Friday deal at $0.99/month, and that promo period just ended, causing the price to jump to full price.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Legitimate charge from Hulu.

                Honestly, most of these are fine. Hulu is a massive, legitimate company owned by Disney. It's very rare for this to be a scam. Usually, it's just a subscription you forgot about. Don't panic yet — check your email for a "Welcome" message from Hulu and monthly receipts. Ask family members if they've been watching anything on the Hulu app. If you still can't find the account, it's possible your card was used by someone else, but check your own history first.
