# What is MSFT * on my credit card statement?

                This is a charge from Microsoft for a subscription or one-time purchase, commonly caused by auto-renewals for Microsoft 365, Office, OneDrive, Xbox Game Pass, or Windows Store purchases.

## Full Name & What It Is

            The letters MSFT stand for Microsoft. It is the official shorthand and stock ticker symbol for the technology company. The asterisk (*) works as a separator — the text after it usually represents a specific transaction ID or product. Common charges include Microsoft 365 Family ($99.99/year), Microsoft 365 Personal ($69.99/year), OneDrive storage plans ($1.99-$2.99/month), and Xbox subscriptions. Many users see this charge because they forgot about an auto-renewing subscription set up months or years ago.

                Merchant Code: MSFT *

                Company: Microsoft Corporation

                Service: Microsoft 365, OneDrive, Xbox, or Windows Store

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Microsoft 365 Auto-Renewal**You signed up for a yearly subscription and it auto-renewed to prevent service interruption.

                **2. OneDrive Free Trial Expiry**You signed up for extra cloud storage and the free trial converted to a paid monthly plan.

                **3. Xbox Game Pass Monthly Plan**You or a family member subscribed to play games and it bills every month until cancelled.

                **4. Windows Store Purchases**You bought a digital app, movie, or in-game content through the Microsoft Store.

                **5. New Laptop Trial**Your new computer came with a free Office trial. You entered your card to set up the device, and the trial converted to paid.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Legitimate charge from Microsoft.

                In almost all cases, MSFT * is a legitimate charge. These charges are commonly disputed because people forget about auto-renewals. Check if a family member used your card, or if a trial on a new computer expired. If you've checked every account and are certain no one made the purchase, contact your bank immediately.

## How to Get a Refund

                **Step 1.** Sign in at account.microsoft.com with the email linked to the billing charge.
                **Step 2.** Go to "Services & Subscriptions" to see all active plans.
                **Step 3.** Select the subscription, click "Manage," then "Cancel subscription." If within the grace period, you'll be offered a full refund.
                **Step 4.** If online refund fails, call 1-877-696-7786. If still unresolved, dispute with your bank.

## Frequently Asked Questions

                    Why is Microsoft charging my credit card?
                    This usually happens due to an active, auto-renewing subscription like Microsoft 365 or OneDrive. It can also be a purchase made through the Windows Store by you or a family member. You can check your full purchase history by signing in to account.microsoft.com.

                    Can I get a refund for an accidental MSFT * charge?
                    Yes, Microsoft often issues automatic refunds for yearly subscriptions if you cancel them within 30 days of the renewal date. To request your money back, sign in to your Microsoft dashboard and look for the cancellation options under your subscriptions. If that fails, you can contact their support phone line at 1-877-696-7786.

                    Does MSFT * cover services like LinkedIn or GitHub?
                    No, those platforms are billed under their own separate descriptors on your credit card statement. Even though Microsoft owns LinkedIn and GitHub, their charges show up with different codes and names. This helps you easily distinguish them from standard Microsoft software and game purchases.
