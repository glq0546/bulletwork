# What is PYPL on my credit card statement?

                If you see a charge on your card statement starting with PYPL, it's a charge from PayPal for a purchase you made through their platform. PayPal is just the middleman here — you bought something from a business or a person online and chose to pay using your PayPal account.

## Full Name & What It Is

            PYPL is simply the short abbreviation and official stock ticker symbol for PayPal. When you use PayPal to pay for something online, the charge on your bank statement displays "PYPL" followed by the merchant's name. PayPal is the middleman — your bank sends the cash to PayPal, then PayPal passes it along to the seller. You might see this for a simple online order, a monthly subscription, or even cash you sent to a friend.

                Merchant Code: PYPL

                Company: PayPal, Inc.

                Service: Payment processor for online purchases and peer-to-peer transfers

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Online Shopping with PayPal Checkout**You bought a handmade shelf on Etsy and used the PayPal button, so the charge shows as PYPL *ETSY on your statement.

                **2. Recurring Subscriptions**You pay $4.99/month to support a podcast on Patreon, and the auto-payment shows as PYPL *PATREON.

                **3. Sending Money to Friends**You paid your friend back for dinner last night using your phone, and the money came out of your linked credit card.

                **4. Small Online Shops**You ordered a laptop charger from a small tech shop that uses PayPal's checkout system to process payments.

                **5. Gaming & Community Subscriptions**Your monthly Discord Nitro subscription billed your card through PayPal.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Usually legitimate.

                Honestly, most of these are fine. It's usually a purchase you forgot about or a store using a different corporate name on their billing details. But scammers do love PayPal because it's so quick to set up. Don't panic yet — log into your PayPal account, check your transaction history for the exact date and amount. If you find nothing at all, someone might've swiped your card info or gotten into your account. Open a dispute in PayPal's Resolution Center immediately.
