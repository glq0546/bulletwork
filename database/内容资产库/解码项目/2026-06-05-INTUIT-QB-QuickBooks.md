# What is INTUIT *QB on my credit card statement?

                This is a charge from Intuit for a QuickBooks subscription, accounting software used by small businesses, freelancers, and self-employed people to manage their finances.

## Full Name & What It Is

            "INTUIT" refers to Intuit Inc., the financial software company that also owns TurboTax and Mailchimp. "*QB" is shorthand for QuickBooks, the world's most popular accounting software for small to medium-sized businesses. QuickBooks is now a subscription service — you pay monthly or yearly to keep using it. Common plans include Simple Start (~$30/mo), Essentials (~$60/mo), Plus (~$90/mo), and Advanced ($200+/mo). Many people see this charge because they signed up for a free 30-day trial and forgot to cancel.

                Merchant Code: INTUIT *QB

                Company: Intuit Inc.

                Service: QuickBooks Accounting Software Subscription

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Freelancer or Side-Hustler**You signed up for QuickBooks Solopreneur or Self-Employed to track mileage and expenses. TurboTax often links to QuickBooks during tax season.

                **2. Free Trial Expired**You signed up for "30 days free" and stopped using the app but didn't hit "cancel." Billing started automatically.

                **3. Annual Subscription Renewal**A large charge ($300-$600) likely means an annual plan renewed. These hit once a year and are easy to forget.

                **4. Your Accountant or Bookkeeper**A professional may have set up a QuickBooks file in your name and used your card for billing.

                **5. Payroll Add-On**If you have employees, QuickBooks charges extra per employee for payroll processing.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Legitimate charge from Intuit.

                Intuit is a multi-billion dollar corporation. This is not a criminal scam. But many users feel scammed because the billing can be confusing and the charges are higher than typical subscriptions. If you truly have no connection to QuickBooks, have never used TurboTax, and don't have an accountant, then someone may have used your card — contact your bank immediately.
