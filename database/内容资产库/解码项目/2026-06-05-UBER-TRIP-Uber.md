# What is UBER TRIP on my credit card statement?

                A charge from UBER TRIP is from Uber, meaning you paid for a ride, an Uber Eats delivery, or a temporary authorization hold.

## Full Name & What It Is

            UBER TRIP stands for Uber. It shows up when you pay for a ride or an Uber Eats order through their app. You might see different types of charges. These include instant rides, scheduled rides, food deliveries, driver tips, and surge pricing during busy times.

            You might notice a weird $1 charge. Don't panic yet. This is just a temporary authorization hold. Uber checks that your card works before the ride starts. This hold drops off your statement in a few days. It is not a real charge.

            Your final bill can also be higher than the upfront estimate. This happens if your driver hits traffic or you change the drop-off spot. If you take a ride in another country, you might see a currency conversion fee from your bank on top of the Uber fare.

                Merchant Code: UBER TRIP

                Company: Uber Technologies, Inc.

                Service: Uber Ride, Uber Eats Delivery, or Temporary Authorization Hold

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Late Night Ride**You took a ride on a late weekend night and forgot about it.

                **2. Uber Eats Delivery**You ordered dinner through Uber Eats.

                **3. Family Member Used Your Card**A family member used your saved card on their phone.

                **4. $1 Temporary Hold**You see a $1 temporary hold that will disappear soon.

                **5. Foreign Currency Ride**You traveled abroad and paid for an Uber in a foreign currency.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Legitimate charge from Uber.

                Honestly, Uber is a legit company, not a scam. But surprise charges still feel awful. You should open your app and check your trip history. See if the dates and amounts match up. ## Frequently Asked Questions

                    Why did UBER TRIP charge me $1?
                    This $1 charge is a temporary authorization hold. Uber uses it to verify your card is active before you request a ride. It will drop off your bank statement in a few days.

                    Why is my Uber charge more than the estimate?
                    Your final price changes if your trip takes longer than expected due to traffic or route changes. Surge pricing can also increase the cost during busy times.

                    How do I get a refund from Uber?
                    Open the app, select "Your Trips," and find the ride you want to dispute. Tap "Help" on that trip to request a fare review or a refund directly from Uber.
