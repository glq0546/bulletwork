# What is DRI* on my credit card statement?

                If you see a charge on your card statement starting with DRI*, it's a charge from Digital River, a payment processor that handles subscriptions and purchases for software companies. The name after DRI* is usually the software or service you bought.

## Full Name & What It Is

            DRI stands for Digital River. They're a behind-the-scenes payment company that many software brands use to handle their billing. When you buy antivirus software, a PDF editor, or a cloud backup service, the actual company might use Digital River to process the payment. That's why you see DRI* on your statement instead of the software company's name. Digital River is the middleman — they make selling downloads and managing auto-renewals easier for software developers.

                Merchant Code: DRI*

                Company: Digital River, Inc.

                Service: Payment processor for software subscriptions and digital product purchases

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Antivirus Auto-Renewal**You bought Kaspersky or Avast antivirus software last year, and your subscription just auto-renewed for another twelve months through Digital River's billing system.

                **2. PDF Editor License**You purchased a license for a PDF editing tool like Nitro PDF or Nuance, and the card transaction went through Digital River.

                **3. Cloud Backup Service**You set up a monthly cloud backup service with Carbonite or a similar brand, and they billed your card through their partner platform.

                **4. Digital Game Downloads**You paid for a digital game or expansion pack from a publisher like Square Enix or Capcom, and the charge looks like DRI*SQUARE ENIX on your statement.

                **5. Work Software Tools**You bought a design program or utility tool for your work computer, and the receipt came from Digital River instead of the developer.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Usually legitimate.

                Honestly, most of these are fine. It's usually a purchase you forgot about or an auto-renewal for a program you installed months ago. Don't panic yet — search your email inbox for "Digital River" or "DRI" to find a receipt. Check your computer for any antivirus programs, backup software, or utility apps. These often renew automatically once a year. If you've searched everywhere and still can't find anything, someone might've used your card without your permission.
