# What is GODADDY.COM on my credit card statement?

                A charge from GODADDY.COM is for a domain, website hosting, or another online service through GoDaddy. You probably bought a domain a few years ago and forgot about the auto-renewal.

## Full Name & What It Is

            GODADDY.COM stands for GoDaddy. They are one of the world's biggest domain registrars and web hosting companies. You see this charge when you buy stuff from them. Common products include domain registrations, web hosting, SSL certificates, and Microsoft 365 email.

            Prices look cheap at first. You might pay $0.99 to $19.99 for your first year of a domain. When it renews, the price jumps to $25 to $80 a year. Web hosting runs $5.99 to $24.99 a month. SSL certificates cost $63.99 to $149.99 a year.

            GoDaddy turns on auto-renewal by default. That cheap first-year deal turns into a huge bill later. They also add "domain privacy protection" as a separate auto-renewing charge. It is easy to miss this extra fee.

                Merchant Code: GODADDY.COM

                Company: GoDaddy Inc.

                Service: Domain Registration, Web Hosting, SSL Certificate, or Email Subscription

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Expired Domain Renewal**You grabbed a $0.99 domain for a side project two years ago, forgot about it, and just got hit with a $40 renewal.

                **2. Web Hosting Auto-Renew**Your web hosting plan renewed automatically at the standard monthly rate.

                **3. SSL Certificate Renewal**An SSL certificate expired and auto-renewed at full price.

                **4. Former Employee's Purchase**A former employee bought a domain for your business on your company card, and it's still renewing.

                **5. Domain Privacy Fee**You got charged for both your domain and a separate "domain privacy" fee.

## Is This a Scam?

                ⚠️ Legitimate, but auto-renewal feels sneaky.

                Honestly, GoDaddy is a real company, not a scam. But their auto-renewal policy makes people feel tricked. You agree to a cheap price, then get billed way more later without a clear reminder.

                Don't panic yet. Go to your GoDaddy account and check your product list. Look at the billing history. You will likely spot the exact service that charged you.
