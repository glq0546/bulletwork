# What is POS DEBIT on my credit card statement?

                POS DEBIT is a charge from an in-person transaction where you used your card at a physical store's payment terminal. POS stands for Point of Sale, and DEBIT means the money was taken directly from your account.

## Full Name & What It Is

            POS DEBIT stands for Point of Sale Debit. This label appears whenever you go to a physical location and swipe, insert, or tap your card to pay for something. You'll see this at grocery stores, gas stations, restaurants, or small retail shops. Even if you used a credit card, the merchant's system might categorize it this way if they processed it through a specific debit network. The merchant's name often gets cut off — you might only see "POS DEBIT" followed by numbers or a city name.

                Merchant Code: POS DEBIT

                Type: Point of Sale Transaction

                Service: In-person card payment at a physical store terminal

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Gas Station Stop**You stopped at a small independent gas station on a road trip and paid at the pump for a full tank.

                **2. Farmers Market Vendor**You visited a local farmers market and used your card at a vendor's handheld tablet to buy fresh produce and honey.

                **3. Busy Downtown Deli**You grabbed a quick lunch at a deli where the receipt shows a different corporate name than the sign on the door.

                **4. Salon or Barbershop**You paid for a haircut at a salon that uses a generic payment processor, making the charge show up with just a phone number or store ID.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Usually legitimate.

                Honestly, most of these are fine. It's normal to feel worried when you don't recognize a name on your statement, but don't panic yet. Start by checking your receipts — match the dollar amount and date. Look at the location next to the charge. Think about where you were that day. Did you grab coffee? Buy gum at the pharmacy? If you still aren't sure, Google the numbers after "POS DEBIT" — they're often just a store ID that leads to the real business name.

## Customer Service

                Important: There is no universal phone number for POS DEBIT charges. This is a general label used by thousands of different stores, so there isn't one central office to call.

                If you have a question about a specific charge, contact the merchant where you made the purchase. If you can't find the merchant or you're sure the charge is wrong, call the number on the back of your card to speak with your bank.

## Frequently Asked Questions

                    Why does my credit card show a POS DEBIT charge?
                    This happens when you use your card at a physical payment terminal in a store. The store's system processes it as a point-of-sale transaction, which labels it as POS DEBIT on your statement.

                    How do I find out who charged me for a POS DEBIT?
                    Look at the date, the amount, and any city or store ID numbers next to the charge. You can often match this to a receipt or your location history from that day to find the merchant.

                    Can I dispute a POS DEBIT charge?
                    Yes, you can dispute the charge if you didn't make the purchase or if the amount is wrong. Try to talk to the merchant first, but if that doesn't work, contact your bank to start a dispute.
