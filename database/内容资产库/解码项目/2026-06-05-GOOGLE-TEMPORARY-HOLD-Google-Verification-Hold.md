# What is GOOGLE *TEMPORARY HOLD on my credit card statement?

                This is not a real charge. It's a pending verification hold that Google uses to confirm your credit card is active and valid, and it will disappear from your statement automatically, usually within 1 to 7 business days.

## Full Name & What It Is

            The line item GOOGLE *TEMPORARY HOLD is a security check called a pre-authorization hold. It is not an actual debit. Google places a small temporary hold (usually $0.00 or $1.00) when you add a new card, sign up for a free trial, or make your first purchase. The hold verifies your card is valid and has available funds.

            There's an important difference: a Pending Charge is a temporary hold — your bank reserves the money but it never leaves your account. A Posted Charge is a settled transaction where money has actually moved. A GOOGLE *TEMPORARY HOLD will always stay in the "pending" section and will never become a posted charge.

            Do not confuse this with real Google subscriptions like YouTube Premium, Google One, or Google Workspace. Those are actual charges that post to your account. The temporary hold is strictly for card verification.

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Adding a New Payment Method**When you link a new card to your Google account or Google Pay, Google tests it immediately to verify the details are correct.

                **2. Starting a Free Trial**Signing up for YouTube Premium, Google One, or Fitbit Premium triggers a verification. The trial is free, but Google checks your card is valid for when billing begins.

                **3. First Google Play Purchase**Buying an app, book, or movie on the Play Store for the first time may trigger a verification hold alongside your purchase.

                **4. Setting Up Google Pay**Activating Google Pay on a new phone or smartwatch tests your connected cards.

                **5. Booking Travel via Google**Using Google Travel to book a hotel, flight, or rental car may place a hold to secure the booking.

## Is This a Scam?

                ✅ Legitimate verification hold from Google.

                A genuine GOOGLE *TEMPORARY HOLD is not fraud — it's a standard security measure. Scammers occasionally use similar names like "GOOGLE *TEMP" to trick people. If the hold is for a large amount ($50+) or you don't have a Google account at all, contact your bank immediately.

## What to Do (It's Not a Refund)

            Because this is a temporary hold, not a real charge, you don't need a refund — no money actually left your account. Just follow these steps:

                **Step 1.** Wait 1-7 business days. The hold will automatically disappear. You usually don't need to do anything.
                **Step 2.** If it's been more than 7 days, check payments.google.com → Activity tab to review recent transactions.
                **Step 3.** Contact your bank. Ask if the item is still "pending" or has fully "posted" as a real transaction.
                **Step 4.** If the bank confirms it posted as a real debit you don't recognize, file a dispute for an unauthorized charge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

                    Will GOOGLE *TEMPORARY HOLD charge my bank account?
                    No, this is not an actual charge. It is a temporary pre-authorization check used to test if your credit card is active. The pending amount will be released automatically and will never be deducted from your final account balance.

                    How long does it take for a Google temporary hold to disappear?
                    Most banks will remove the temporary hold within 1 to 7 business days. In some cases, depending on your bank's specific policies, it may take up to 14 business days for the pending line item to fully clear from your transaction history.

                    Can I call someone to remove a pending hold immediately?
                    Google cannot manually remove a pending hold once it has been initiated. You must wait for your credit card issuer to release the held funds automatically. If the hold does not clear after a week, you can call Google support at 1-855-836-3987 or contact your bank's customer service team for help.
