# What is ADOBE CREATIVE CLOUD on my credit card statement?

                This is a charge from Adobe for a Creative Cloud subscription, giving you access to apps like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro. You're probably paying monthly or yearly, and it likely auto-renewed.

## Full Name & What It Is

            ADOBE CREATIVE CLOUD is the official billing name for Adobe's suite of creative software. Plans: Photography plan ($9.99/mo for Photoshop + Lightroom), Single App ($22.99/mo), All Apps ($59.99/mo for everything Adobe makes). Critical warning: Adobe uses an "annual plan paid monthly" model. You're signing a 12-month contract, not a monthly service you can cancel anytime. If you cancel after 14 days, Adobe charges 50% of your remaining contract as a penalty. With six months left on a $60 plan, that's $180 just to close your account.

                Merchant Code: ADOBE CREATIVE CLOUD

                Company: Adobe Inc.

                Service: Creative Cloud Subscription (annual contract, paid monthly)

## Common Scenarios

                **1. Free Trial Forgotten**You needed to edit one photo for a wedding gift, signed up for a free trial of Photoshop, and forgot to cancel before the week was up.

                **2. Student Discount Expired**You got a student discount for a class, but now the semester is over and you're still being billed at the student rate.

                **3. Monthly Payment = Annual Contract**You wanted Premiere Pro for a quick video, not realizing "monthly payments" locked you into a 12-month contract with a hefty cancellation penalty.

                **4. Former Employee's Subscription**You provided a company card for an employee who has since left, but the Creative Cloud subscription is still active.

                **5. Promo Price Jumped**Your introductory rate ended, and the price jumped to full retail without you noticing.

## Is This a Scam?

                ⚠️ Legitimate, but feels sneaky.

                Honestly, Adobe is a legitimate company and isn't stealing your money like a hacker would. But there's a reason so many people feel scammed. The way Adobe hides the 12-month commitment in fine print feels very sneaky. When you try to cancel and see a $100+ penalty, it's natural to feel angry. Don't panic yet — log into your Adobe account and look at "Plans and payment" to see your contract end date. Check any other email addresses you might have used too.

## Frequently Asked Questions

                    Why is Adobe charging me a cancellation fee?
                    Most Adobe plans are 12-month contracts, even if you pay monthly. If you cancel after the first 14 days, Adobe charges a fee equal to 50% of your remaining contract balance.

                    How do I stop ADOBE CREATIVE CLOUD charges?
                    Log into your Adobe account and cancel the subscription under the "Plans" menu. Simply uninstalling the apps from your computer will not stop the monthly billing.

                    Can I get a refund from Adobe?
                    Adobe offers a full refund if you cancel within 14 days of your initial purchase. Past that window, contact support at 1-800-833-6687 to ask for a special exception.
