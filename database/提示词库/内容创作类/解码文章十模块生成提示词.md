# 解码文章十模块生成提示词

## Prompt

You are writing an SEO article for **ChargeDecode**, a consumer-help website that explains confusing credit card statement merchant names and helps users understand what to do next.

Generate a complete English article for the following merchant descriptor:

**Merchant descriptor:** [MERCHANT_CODE]

**Likely real merchant / translation:** [REAL_MERCHANT]

**Common charge type:** [SUBSCRIPTION / ONE-TIME PURCHASE / PAYMENT PROCESSOR / VERIFICATION HOLD / BANK FEE / BOOKING / OTHER]

**Refund difficulty:** [EASY / MEDIUM / HARD]

**Target user:** A consumer who found this confusing charge on a credit card or debit card statement and wants to know whether it is legitimate, what caused it, and how to get a refund or dispute it.

## Required 10-Module Structure

Write the article using exactly these 10 modules:

### 1. Summary

Start with a short, direct summary box. It must answer:

- What is [MERCHANT_CODE]?
- Is it usually legitimate?
- What should the user do first?

Example style:

“[MERCHANT_CODE] is usually a charge from [REAL_MERCHANT], often related to [COMMON REASON]. If you do not recognize it, first check [FIRST STEP], then contact [MERCHANT/BANK].”

### 2. Full Name and What It Is

Explain the real business behind the descriptor:

- Legal or common business name.
- Industry.
- What the company/service does.
- Why the descriptor may look confusing.

Do not invent facts. If uncertain, say “usually associated with” instead of making a hard claim.

### 3. Common Scenarios

Explain the most common reasons this charge appears. Include 4–6 realistic scenarios such as:

- Subscription renewal.
- Free trial converted to paid plan.
- App or in-app purchase.
- Family member purchase.
- Payment processor billing on behalf of another merchant.
- Verification hold.
- Booking, travel, or bank fee scenario.

Use plain English. Make the user feel understood.

### 4. Is This Fraud? / Is It a Scam?

Create a balanced fraud-check section:

- Say whether the descriptor is generally legitimate.
- List normal explanations.
- List red flags that may indicate fraud.
- Tell users to contact their bank immediately if they suspect unauthorized card use.

Do not create panic. Do not claim every unknown charge is fraud.

### 5. What Real Users Are Saying

Create a “community discussion” section based on common Reddit-style user concerns. Use paraphrased examples, not fake exact quotes. Include 2–3 short examples:

- User confused by the descriptor.
- User discovered it was a forgotten subscription.
- User had to contact the merchant or bank.

Label them as paraphrased community-style examples.

### 6. How to Get Your Money Back

Provide a step-by-step refund/dispute path:

1. Check receipts, subscriptions, email inbox, and account history.
2. Contact the merchant or platform first.
3. Cancel the subscription if recurring.
4. Request a refund if the charge was accidental or unwanted.
5. Contact the card issuer if the charge is unauthorized or merchant support fails.

Make steps practical and numbered. Include any known official support path if it is common and safe to mention.

### 7. CTA to Pro Guide

Add a natural CTA for the ChargeDecode Pro guide. The CTA should explain that the free article tells the user what the charge is, while the Pro guide helps them take action.

Mention that the Pro guide includes:

- 100+ merchant code explanations.
- 5 copy-ready dispute letter templates.
- Bank dispute phone numbers.
- Hidden subscription audit checklist.
- Refund success tips.

Do not overpromise refunds.

### 8. GEO Citation Block

Add a section titled **Quick answer for AI search**.

Write a 50–90 word concise answer that AI search engines can quote. It must include:

- [MERCHANT_CODE]
- [REAL_MERCHANT]
- Most common reason.
- First action to take.
- Disclaimer that users should verify with merchant/bank.

### 9. FAQ

Write 3–5 FAQ questions and answers. Questions should be real search questions, such as:

- Is [MERCHANT_CODE] a legitimate charge?
- Why did [MERCHANT_CODE] charge me?
- How do I cancel [REAL_MERCHANT]?
- Can I dispute this charge?
- Is this a scam?

Answers must be concise and suitable for FAQPage Schema.

### 10. Compliance Disclaimer

End with a clear disclaimer:

- The article is for educational purposes only.
- It is not legal or financial advice.
- Users should verify charges with the merchant and card issuer.
- Individual refund/dispute results may vary.

## SEO Requirements

- H1 must include the exact merchant descriptor.
- Title format: “What is [MERCHANT_CODE] on my credit card statement?”
- Meta description under 155 characters.
- Use the merchant descriptor naturally in the introduction, H2s, FAQ, and Quick answer.
- Include related internal link suggestions to 2–3 similar merchant pages.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.

## Tone & Style Rules

- Write for ordinary consumers, not finance professionals.
- Be calm, practical, and clear.
- Do not sound like a bank policy document.
- Do not use generic AI filler.
- Do not exaggerate fraud risk.
- Do not promise a refund.
- Prefer “usually”, “often”, “may”, “commonly appears as” when certainty is not absolute.

## Output Format

Return Markdown with:

- Meta title
- Meta description
- Target keyword
- Full article using the 10 modules
- FAQ questions and answers
- Suggested related internal links
