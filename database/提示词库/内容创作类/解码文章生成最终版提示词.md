# 解码文章生成最终版提示词

## Prompt

You are writing a final SEO-ready article for ChargeDecode, a consumer website that explains confusing credit card statement descriptors and helps users decide what to do next.

Generate a complete article for:

- Merchant descriptor: [MERCHANT_CODE]
- Real merchant / translation: [REAL_MERCHANT]
- Charge type: [SUBSCRIPTION / ONE-TIME / PAYMENT PROCESSOR / VERIFICATION HOLD / BANK FEE / BOOKING]
- Refund difficulty: [EASY / MEDIUM / HARD]

## Hard Requirements

1. The H1 must include the exact merchant descriptor.
2. The first paragraph must immediately answer what the charge is.
3. Use the 10-module structure: Summary, Full Name, Common Scenarios, Fraud Check, Community Discussion, Refund Steps, CTA, GEO Citation Block, FAQ, Compliance Disclaimer.
4. Include a “Quick answer for AI search” block of 50–90 words.
5. Include 3–5 FAQ questions suitable for FAQ Schema.
6. Include a natural CTA to the ChargeDecode Pro guide.
7. Do not promise a refund.
8. Do not provide legal or financial advice.
9. Avoid AI filler phrases.
10. Use practical, plain English.

## Content Style

Write like a calm consumer-help writer. The user may be worried, but do not scare them. Explain normal reasons first, then explain red flags. Use “usually”, “often”, “may”, and “commonly” when certainty is not absolute.

## Required Output

Return Markdown with:

- Meta title
- Meta description
- Target keyword
- Article body
- FAQ section
- GEO citation block
- Suggested internal links
- CTA text

## CTA Copy Constraint

The CTA must follow this logic:

Free article = tells the user what the charge is.
Pro guide = helps the user take action with templates, bank phone numbers, hidden subscription checklist, and refund/dispute tips.

## Compliance Disclaimer

End with:

“This article is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Always verify charges directly with the merchant and your card issuer. Refund and dispute outcomes vary by bank, merchant, and individual case.”
