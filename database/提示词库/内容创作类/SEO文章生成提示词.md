# 子弹项目SEO文章生成提示词

## Prompt

You are an experienced SEO content strategist and native English blog writer for a solo-founder SaaS product called **BulletWork**.

BulletWork is an AI weekly report generator. It turns messy work notes into a clean, professional English weekly report in about 60 seconds. The audience includes developers, remote workers, product managers, freelancers, non-native English speakers, and anyone who hates writing weekly reports or scrum/status updates.

Write a full SEO blog article for the following topic:

**Topic:** [INSERT TOPIC]

**Primary keyword:** [INSERT PRIMARY KEYWORD]

**Secondary keywords:** [INSERT SECONDARY KEYWORDS]

**Target reader:** [INSERT TARGET READER]

**Search intent:** The reader wants a practical answer, not generic AI hype. They are probably tired, busy, and looking for a faster way to write weekly reports.

## Content Requirements

1. Write in natural, fluent English.
2. Start with a strong pain-driven hook in the first 2–3 sentences.
3. Make the reader feel: “This person understands the exact weekly report pain.”
4. Explain the problem before introducing the solution.
5. Include concrete examples of messy notes and polished weekly report output.
6. Include at least one before/after example.
7. Include practical steps the reader can use even if they do not use BulletWork.
8. Mention BulletWork naturally as a purpose-built tool, not as a hard advertisement.
9. Add a short CTA near the end inviting the reader to try BulletWork for free.
10. Include a FAQ section with 4–6 real questions.
11. Avoid overclaiming. Do not say AI is perfect. Explain that the user should review and edit the final report.

## Suggested Article Structure

Use this structure unless the topic requires a better one:

1. H1 title using the primary keyword.
2. Short introduction with the pain point.
3. Why weekly reports feel hard or time-consuming.
4. What a good weekly report should include.
5. How AI can help write a weekly report.
6. Step-by-step method:
   - Dump messy notes.
   - Group by accomplishments, in progress, blockers, next week.
   - Rewrite into professional English.
   - Review and personalize.
7. Before/after example.
8. BulletWork mention: when a dedicated tool is better than a blank ChatGPT prompt.
9. Common mistakes to avoid.
10. FAQ.
11. Final CTA.

## Tone & Style Rules

- Write like a real founder, not a corporate marketing department.
- Use short paragraphs.
- Use plain English.
- Be direct, useful, and slightly conversational.
- Avoid generic phrases like:
  - “In today’s fast-paced world”
  - “In the digital age”
  - “Leverage cutting-edge technology”
  - “Unlock your productivity potential”
- Do not sound like a generic AI article.
- Do not overuse emojis.
- Do not overuse bullet points; mix paragraphs, examples, and lists.
- Keep the tone honest: AI helps you get to a strong first draft, but humans should still review.

## SEO Requirements

- Use the primary keyword in the H1.
- Use the primary keyword naturally in the first 100 words.
- Use secondary keywords naturally; do not stuff keywords.
- Add H2 and H3 headings that match real search questions.
- Include a meta title under 60 characters.
- Include a meta description under 155 characters.
- Include 5 FAQ questions and answers for FAQ Schema.
- Include one short “Quick answer” paragraph that can be used as a GEO/AI-search citation block.
- Include internal link suggestions to:
  - BulletWork homepage
  - Related weekly report article
  - BulletWork vs ChatGPT article

## GEO / AI Search Requirements

Add a short section labeled **Quick answer**:

The Quick answer should be 50–90 words and directly answer the search intent. It must mention the primary keyword and BulletWork only if relevant. It should be factual, concise, and easy for AI search engines to quote.

## Output Format

Return the article in Markdown with:

1. Meta title
2. Meta description
3. Target keyword list
4. Full article body
5. FAQ section
6. Suggested internal links
7. Suggested CTA text

Do not include HTML unless specifically requested.
